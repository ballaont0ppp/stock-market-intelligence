# Participant Recruitment Guide

## Overview

This guide outlines the process for recruiting and preparing participants for usability testing of the Stock Portfolio Management Platform.

## Recruitment Criteria

### Target Participants

We need **5-10 participants** with diverse backgrounds:

#### Experience Levels
- **Novice (2-3 participants)**: No stock trading experience
- **Intermediate (3-4 participants)**: Some trading experience
- **Expert (2-3 participants)**: Regular traders

#### Technical Proficiency
- **Low (2 participants)**: Basic computer skills
- **Medium (4-5 participants)**: Comfortable with web applications
- **High (2-3 participants)**: Tech-savvy users

#### Demographics
- Age range: 18-65
- Mix of genders
- Various educational backgrounds
- Different occupations

## Recruitment Sources

### 1. Internal Recruitment
- Team members (not involved in development)
- Colleagues from other departments
- Friends and family

**Pros**: Easy to recruit, no cost
**Cons**: May be biased, familiar with team

### 2. Online Recruitment Platforms
- UserTesting.com
- Respondent.io
- User Interviews
- Prolific

**Pros**: Professional testers, diverse pool
**Cons**: Cost involved ($50-100 per participant)

### 3. Social Media
- LinkedIn posts
- Facebook groups (finance, investing)
- Twitter/X
- Reddit (r/investing, r/stocks)

**Pros**: Free, targeted audience
**Cons**: Time-consuming, variable quality

### 4. University Partnerships
- Business school students
- Computer science students
- Finance clubs

**Pros**: Motivated participants, diverse
**Cons**: May lack real-world experience

## Recruitment Process

### Step 1: Create Screening Survey

Use Google Forms or SurveyMonkey to screen participants:

**Questions:**
1. What is your age range?
   - 18-25
   - 26-35
   - 36-45
   - 46-55
   - 56-65
   - 65+

2. Do you have experience with stock trading?
   - No experience
   - Some experience (1-2 years)
   - Experienced (3+ years)

3. How would you rate your technical proficiency?
   - Low (basic computer skills)
   - Medium (comfortable with web apps)
   - High (tech-savvy)

4. Have you used online trading platforms before?
   - Yes
   - No

5. Which platforms have you used? (if applicable)
   - Robinhood
   - E*TRADE
   - TD Ameritrade
   - Fidelity
   - Other: ___________

6. Are you available for a 60-minute usability testing session?
   - Yes
   - No

7. Preferred testing format:
   - In-person
   - Remote (Zoom/Teams)

8. Email address for follow-up:

### Step 2: Select Participants

Review responses and select participants to achieve:
- Balanced experience levels
- Diverse technical proficiency
- Mix of demographics
- Availability alignment

## Consent and Ethics

### Informed Consent Form

Create a consent form covering:
- Purpose of the study
- What participants will do
- How data will be used
- Privacy and confidentiality
- Right to withdraw
- Recording consent
- Signature and date

**Template available in:** `usability-testing/CONSENT_FORM.md`

### Privacy Considerations

- Use participant IDs, not names
- Anonymize all data
- Secure storage of recordings
- Delete recordings after analysis
- GDPR/privacy law compliance

## Scheduling Tips

### Best Practices

1. **Offer Multiple Time Slots**
   - Weekday mornings
   - Weekday afternoons
   - Weekday evenings
   - Weekend options

2. **Buffer Time**
   - 15 minutes between sessions
   - Account for technical issues
   - Allow for overruns

3. **Confirmation Reminders**
   - Send reminder 24 hours before
   - Send reminder 2 hours before
   - Include all details again

4. **Backup Participants**
   - Recruit 1-2 extra participants
   - Have backup slots available
   - Account for no-shows (10-20%)

## Participant Preparation

### Pre-Session Checklist

Send to participants 24 hours before:

- [ ] Test internet connection
- [ ] Test microphone/camera
- [ ] Close unnecessary applications
- [ ] Have water/coffee ready
- [ ] Find quiet location
- [ ] Review consent form
- [ ] Prepare questions (if any)

### Day-of Checklist

- [ ] Join 5 minutes early
- [ ] Have participant ID ready
- [ ] Relax and be yourself
- [ ] Think aloud during tasks
- [ ] Ask questions if confused
- [ ] Provide honest feedback

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

**Participant doesn't show up:**
- Wait 10 minutes
- Send email/text reminder
- Reschedule if needed
- Use backup participant

**Technical difficulties:**
- Have backup device ready
- Test Zoom/recording tools beforehand
- Have IT support contact ready
- Be patient and flexible

**Participant is nervous:**
- Reassure them there are no wrong answers
- Emphasize we're testing the system, not them
- Start with easy tasks
- Build rapport first

**Participant is too experienced:**
- Still valuable feedback
- Focus on expert insights
- Ask about comparisons to other platforms
- Gather suggestions for advanced features
